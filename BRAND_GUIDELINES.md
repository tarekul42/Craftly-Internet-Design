# Craftly Internet: Brand Guidelines

To maintain the premium, technical-editorial identity of the platform, all contributions MUST adhere to these four non-negotiable rules.

## 1. White & Black Only (Strict Monochrome)
- The UI is strictly `#000000` (Black) and `#FFFFFF` (White).
- **No semantic colors**: Do not use Red for errors, Green for success, or Yellow for warnings.
- **State Patterns**:
    - **Error**: Double borders (`border-double`), bold typography, or inverted color blocks.
    - **Success**: Inverted black/white chips or subtle pulse animations on monochrome elements.
    - **Warning**: Dashed borders or alpha-based transparency shifts.
- **Imagery**: All images must be treated with a grayscale filter (`filter: grayscale(100%)`).

## 2. Pacifico for Logo Only
- The font **Pacifico** is reserved exclusively for the "Craftly Internet" logo lockup.
- Use **Inter** for body text and headings.
- Use **JetBrains Mono** for technical labels, IDs, and secondary metadata.

## 3. Simplicity over Complexity
- The Information Architecture (IA) must cater to three distinct user types:
    - **Engineer**: Technical depth, audits, and logic maps.
    - **Builder**: Growth, metrics, and application publishing.
    - **Guest**: Discovery, exploration, and community joining.
- Avoid over-engineered terminology for non-technical roles. Use "Publish" instead of "Initialize Node" for Builders.

## 4. Aesthetic & Elegant
- Prioritize whitespace and typographic hierarchy.
- Avoid "noise": Reduce excessive all-caps, micro-labels, and harsh hover transforms.
- Motion should be smooth and deliberate (use `duration-500` or `duration-700` transitions).
